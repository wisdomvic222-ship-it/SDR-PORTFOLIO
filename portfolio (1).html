Subject: Grant for {{org}} — missed this one?
To: {{first_name}} {{last_name}}, {{title}} @ {{org}}
From: [Sender Name], [Company]

Hi {{first_name}},

I noticed {{org}} has been doing strong work in {{program_area}} — your {{recent_initiative}} caught my attention.

We work with organizations like yours to identify and secure grant funding that often gets missed in-house — particularly federal discretionary grants and foundation funding aligned to your work.

Most of our clients find 3–5 viable opportunities in their first research sprint. Happy to do a quick scan for {{org}} at no cost so you can see what's out there before the next deadline window closes.

Worth a 15-minute conversation?

Best,
[Name]

Why it works: Opens with a specific signal (research + named initiative), leads with value (free scan), and uses a low-friction CTA. The personalization variables make each send feel 1:1 even at scale.
Subject: Re: Quick question for {{first_name}}
To: {{first_name}} {{last_name}}, {{title}} @ {{company}}
From: [Sender Name], [Company]

{{first_name}},

Circling back on my note from last week — I know inboxes are brutal.

One thing I didn't mention: teams in {{industry}} using our platform typically cut manual prospecting time by 60% in the first 30 days. That's time back for actual selling.

I'll keep it short — do you have 10 minutes Thursday or Friday this week?

Either way, I can send over a quick breakdown of how we work with {{company_type}} teams if that's easier.

[Name]

Why it works: Short, punchy follow-up that adds new value (the 60% stat) instead of just "bumping" the thread. Two-option CTA reduces decision fatigue.
Subject: Closing the loop, {{first_name}}
To: {{first_name}} {{last_name}}, {{title}} @ {{company}}
From: [Sender Name], [Company]

{{first_name}},

I've reached out a few times — I don't want to keep clogging your inbox if the timing isn't right.

I'll take this as a "not now," and I'll close out my notes on {{company}}.

If anything changes — new initiative, funding cycle, or just a better quarter — you know where to find me.

Wishing you a strong rest of the year.

[Name]

Why it works: Break-up emails consistently generate the most replies of any sequence touch. The "closing the loop" framing respects their time while keeping the door open — no guilt, no pressure.

LINKEDIN SALES NAVIGATOR.

How I Build Prospect Lists

1
ICP Filtering: Combine title, seniority, geography, company size, and industry to narrow from millions of profiles to the 200–500 most relevant accounts.
2
Boolean Search: Use AND/OR/NOT operators to layer in niche signals — e.g., "(VP OR Director) AND (Grants OR Funding) NOT (Student)".
3
Account-Level Mapping: Pull multiple contacts per target account — champion, economic buyer, and gatekeeper — so outreach can run in parallel.
4
Export & Enrich: Export leads to Apollo or Hunter.io for email address enrichment, phone verification, and additional firmographic layering before outreach.

My LinkedIn Outreach Framework

Connection requests, InMail, and profile-to-conversation conversion — here's what I've found actually works:

→
Connection Note (under 300 chars): Lead with a relevance signal — a mutual connection, shared content, or a specific observation about their work. Never pitch in the note.
→
Warm-up Message: After connecting, send a value-add message (article, resource, or insight) before any ask. Build credibility first.
→
Soft CTA: The third touchpoint introduces a question or low-friction CTA — never a calendar link in the first message. Curiosity opens doors; calendars close them.
→
InMail for Unreachables: Reserved for senior decision-makers who haven't accepted connections — short, specific, and ends with a binary yes/no question.
